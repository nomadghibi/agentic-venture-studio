## 1. Product Overview

### 1.1 Summary
Agentic Venture Studio is a digital-first software company operated by a coordinated system of specialized AI agents and human oversight. Its purpose is to continuously discover valuable business problems online, validate market demand, assess technical and commercial feasibility, generate product concepts, build MVPs, launch them, and manage them as a portfolio of digital businesses.

Unlike a normal SaaS product, this system does not solve one fixed customer workflow. It is a **meta-company platform** whose output is new product opportunities, validated business cases, and deployable software ventures.

### 1.2 Vision
Create an AI-native venture system that can repeatedly turn internet-observed pain points into monetizable software products with lower time-to-validation, lower build waste, and stronger portfolio decision-making than a traditional startup team.

### 1.3 Mission
Reduce the cost and uncertainty of software venture creation by using agentic workflows to move from idea discovery to product launch in a disciplined, measurable, production-minded way.

## 2. Problem Statement

### 2.1 Current Market Problem
Entrepreneurs, solo founders, agencies, and venture studios face several recurring problems:
- too many ideas, too little structured validation
- weak signal detection from noisy online sources
- building products before proving demand
- overestimating market willingness to pay
- poor technical feasibility assessment early on
- long delays between idea and MVP
- fragmented tooling across research, product, engineering, launch, and analytics
- lack of consistent decision gates for kill/continue/scale choices

### 2.2 Core Pain
Most founders do not fail because they cannot code. They fail because they build the wrong thing, for the wrong user, with weak distribution and poor validation.

### 2.3 Opportunity
A multi-agent system can operate like a disciplined digital venture team:
- sourcing pain points at scale
- scoring opportunities consistently
- mapping buildability and monetization early
- producing standardized product documentation
- generating MVPs faster
- measuring real-world traction
- managing many ideas as a portfolio instead of emotionally

## 3. Goals and Non-Goals

### 3.1 Primary Goals
1. Discover business problems online that show monetization potential.
2. Validate whether the problem is real, frequent, urgent, and worth solving.
3. Determine whether the idea is technically buildable as a software product.
4. Recommend the best business model and pricing path.
5. Produce product artifacts automatically: PRD, architecture, workflows, agent specs, backlog.
6. Generate MVP implementations for selected opportunities.
7. Support launch, measurement, and portfolio-level decision-making.
8. Build a repeatable operating system for idea-to-revenue execution.

### 3.2 Secondary Goals
1. Build a historical knowledge base of rejected and accepted opportunities.
2. Improve future decision quality through evaluation loops.
3. Reduce founder bias in idea selection.
4. Create reusable internal IP around venture design patterns.

### 3.3 Non-Goals
1. Fully autonomous, no-human governance from day one.
2. Replacing legal, accounting, or regulated compliance professionals.
3. Building every idea the system finds.
4. Running complex physical-world businesses directly.
5. Immediate full autonomy in pricing, payroll, capital allocation, or binding contracts.

3. **Small MVP before platform**
4. **Agents reason; deterministic systems execute**

8. **Portfolio thinking beats single-idea emotion**
9. **Production realism over demo theatrics**
10. **Additive architecture and explicit contracts**

## 6. Core User Stories
- As a founder, I want the system to scan online sources for repeated pain signals so I can identify opportunities I would have missed manually.
- As a venture lead, I want opportunities grouped by industry, urgency, and monetization potential.
- As a product strategist, I want evidence-backed validation of a problem so I do not waste time building weak ideas.
- As a technical reviewer, I want feasibility analysis before approving an MVP.
- As a founder, I want the system to transform a validated problem into a PRD, architecture, and MVP roadmap.
- As an engineer, I want build-ready technical artifacts, contracts, and phased delivery scope.
- As a growth lead, I want recommended launch strategies, positioning, and early acquisition channels.
- As an operator, I want to see whether the product is making money and whether to scale or kill it.

## 21. Risks
- false demand from noisy chatter
- overfitting to trend hype
- poor distribution despite strong product logic
- weak scoring model
- low explainability
- hallucinated market analysis
- inadequate oversight

## 22. Risk Mitigations
- require evidence-backed scoring
- separate deterministic scoring from narrative output
- maintain human review at major gates
- store source references
- use typed outputs and schemas
- implement evaluation sets
- start narrow by sector
- enforce kill criteria
